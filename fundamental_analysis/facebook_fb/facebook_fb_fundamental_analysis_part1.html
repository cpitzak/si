Facebook (FB) Fundamental Analysis - Gross Profit Margin

We are going to analyze Facebook (FB) financial statements using the methods described in Warren Buffett and the Interpretation of Financial Statements.

The goal will be to see if Facebook (FB) has a durable competitive advantage according to the methods described in the book. We will use historical data from 2018 to 2010.

Getting the Data

You can get the financial statements from EDGAR Company Filings. On EDGAR in the Fast Search enter the ticker: FB

The Income Statement, Balance Sheet, and Cash Flow data is in the 10-K filings.

Analyzing Gross Profit Margin

As a very general rule (and there are exceptions): Companies with gross profit margins of 40% or better thend to be companies with some sort of durable competitive advantage. page 34 Warren Buffett and the Interpretation of Financial Statements Published: Jan 2011.

Gross Profit Margin = (Revenue - Cost of Goods Sold) / Revenue
