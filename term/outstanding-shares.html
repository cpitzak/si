Outstanding Shares

Outstanding shares are the number of shares that stockholders own.

Outstanding shares can be calculated as basic or fully diluted. The basic count of the outstanding shares is the current number of shares. This number is used when voting in the general meeting of shareholders and for Dividend distributions.

The fully diluted count is all the outstanding shares that will be available after all possible sources of conversion to shares are excercised. Some of these sources include employee stock options, convertiable bonds, capital notes, warrants.

Authorized Shares

The maximum number of shares that can be issued.

Issued Shares

The number of shares the company has issued to its stockholders. Issued shares can never exceed authorized shares.

Float

Float is the amount of shares that are available to the public to buy and sell.

Treasury Shares (aka reacquired shares)

Shares that are bought back by the company. This reduces the amount of outstanding shares.
