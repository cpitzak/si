Price-to-Earnings Ratio - P/E Ratio

The P/E ratio is used to value a company. It's also known as the price multiple or the earnings multiple. It's most widely used among investors. However, many investors believe that the Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) is a better tool for stock valuation.

The P/E is also referred to as the price multiple because it shows how many times $1 earnings an investor is willing to pay. For example, if a company has a P/E ratio of 15 then an investor is willing to pay 15 times $1 earnings. That means an investor is willing to pay $15 for every $1 of earnings.

If earnings and the price of the stock always remained the same then the P/E ratio also shows how many years until the investor will get their money back. For example, for a P/E ratio of 15 it will take 15 years for the company's earnings to pay you back.

Things to know

  • The P/E ratio relates the price of a share to the companies earnings.
  • Companies that are loosing money or have no earnings don't have a P/E ratio because the E is 0 or negative.
  • A high P/E ratio means either that the company is overvalued or that investors expect high earning growth rates.

There are multiple kinds of P/E Ratios. The most popular are:

  • Trailing Twelve Month P/E Ratio (TTM)
  • Forward P/E Ratio
  • P/E Ratio without non-recurring items (NRI)

P/E Ratio (TTM)

The Trailing Twelve Month P/E Ratio (TTM) is calculated as

PE Ratio = Share Price / Earnings per Share (Diluted) (TTM)

P/E Ratio Calculated for Whole Company

It can also be calcuated for the whole company:

PE Ratio = Market Cap / Net Income

Forward P/E Ratio

Forward P/E Ratio is calculated by using the estimated earnings for the next twelve months or the next full-year fiscal period.

PE Ratio = Share Price / Estimated Future Earnings Per Share

P/E Ratio without NRI

Non Operating Income can have an affect on P/E Ratios. An example of Non Operating Income is when part of the busines is sold. Selling part of the business can't occur over and over again. We would like to exclude Non Operating Income items from our P/E ratio calculation so we can have a more accurate valueation.

PE Ratio = Share Price / Earnings Per Share without NRI
