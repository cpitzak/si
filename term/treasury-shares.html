Treasury Shares

Treasury shares are shares that are held in the company's treasury. They come from the company buying back shares that were either part of the outstanding shares or that were shares that were never issued to the public. Treasury shares are recorded in the shareholder's equity section of the balance sheet.

Treasury shares are not included in the EPS calculations and treasury shares have no voting rights. The nation's regulatory body, in the USA this is the Securities and Exchange Commmission (SEC), may limit the amount of treasury shares that can be purchased.

Reasons for Company's Buying back Shares

There are several reasons why a company may want to buy back shares. Here are some of the reasons:

  • Undervalued - If the company is undervalued the company can raise their share price by buying back shares. This gives investors an immediate reward.
  • Taxes - It can be a tax efficient method to put cash into shareholders' hands. When a company pays dividends the stockholder pays capital gains on the dividends received. However, when a company buys back shares, the stockholder doesn't pay any additional tax.
  • Controlling Interest -Buying back shares prevents hostile takeovers. Also, it increases the value of the current shareholders' interest in the company.
  • Retiring Shares - Treasury shares can be retired. The amount that is retired can never again be sold. This permantely reduces the share count which causes the remaining share to represent a larger percentage of the company.
